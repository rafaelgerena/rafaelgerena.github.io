Harvard Business School Podcast: Placing the Per Scholas CEO in the National Future-of-Work Conversation

By Rafael Gerena

Summary: I secured and prepared the Per Scholas CEO for a 35-minute expert interview on the Harvard Business School Managing the Future of Work podcast with Professor Joe Fuller — one of the nation's leading researchers on skills-based hiring and workforce transformation. The placement required developing a full executive briefing covering AI curriculum integration, the Zero Percent Loan program, federal WIOA reauthorization, employer partnerships, and graduate outcome data. It positioned the CEO as a national thought leader on AI adoption and workforce transformation with a global policy, business, and academic audience.

Context

The Harvard Business School Managing the Future of Work podcast, hosted by Professor Joe Fuller, is one of the most credible platforms in the national conversation about skills-based hiring, workforce transformation, and the future of work. Fuller's research on employer hiring practices and the paper ceiling is cited across policy, academic, and business contexts. A placement on this podcast reaches an audience that shapes how organizations think about talent, credentials, and workforce investment — not an audience that consumes workforce development content, but one that funds, designs, and evaluates it.

Securing this placement required understanding what Fuller's research agenda needed — a practitioner who could speak with precision about outcomes, policy, and employer partnerships — and preparing the CEO to deliver exactly that in a 35-minute expert conversation with no margin for vague answers.

The Approach

Executive podcast placements at this level are not media relations transactions. They are thought leadership positioning exercises that require the communications function to do three things well: identify the right platform for the right strategic moment, secure the access, and ensure the executive is prepared to perform at the level the platform demands.

I developed a full executive briefing covering every topic Fuller's research agenda was likely to surface: AI curriculum integration at Per Scholas, the Zero Percent Loan program expanding access to tech training, federal WIOA reauthorization and its implications for workforce funding, employer partnership structures with major technology companies, and graduate outcome data that could withstand academic scrutiny.

The briefing was not talking points. It was a structured preparation document that gave the CEO the depth to speak fluently across policy, program, and outcome dimensions — the three registers a Harvard Business School audience expects simultaneously.

Why It Matters

The Managing the Future of Work audience includes the researchers, policymakers, and corporate leaders who define the terms of the national workforce conversation. A Per Scholas CEO appearance on that platform does not reach job seekers. It reaches the people who decide which workforce organizations get cited in policy briefs, featured in corporate social responsibility strategies, and funded at national scale.

This is what executive communications strategy looks like when it is working: matching the right leader to the right platform at the right moment, and doing the preparation work that makes the conversation earn sustained returns long after the episode airs.
