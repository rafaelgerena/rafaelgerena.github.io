The New York Times Double Placement: Shaping the AI-and-Workforce Narrative Before It Peaked

By Rafael Gerena

Summary: In 2024 and 2025, I secured two New York Times placements for Per Scholas — a Christmas Day editorial board endorsement developed with Pulitzer Prize-winning writer Brent Staples, and a front-page business section feature with technology reporter Steve Lohr framing AI as evolution, not extinction. Both were the result of proactive, foresight-driven outreach initiated before either story existed in its final form.

Results

Placements 2 New York Times placements within 57 days
Placement 1 NYT Editorial Board endorsement — December 25, 2024
Placement 2 NYT Business Section front page — February 20, 2025
Reporter (Placement 2) Steve Lohr, New York Times technology reporter
Writer (Placement 1) Brent Staples, Pulitzer Prize-winning NYT editorial writer
Organization Per Scholas
Annual media impressions 4B+

Context

Per Scholas is one of America's leading AI and tech workforce training organizations, serving 10,000+ learners annually across 25+ markets. In late 2024, the national conversation about AI's impact on workers was forming but had not yet peaked. The organization needed to establish institutional credibility before the debate reached full volume — not after.

Placement 1 — NYT Editorial Board Endorsement, December 25, 2024

I worked directly with Pulitzer Prize-winning New York Times editorial writer Brent Staples and the Times fact-checking team to develop an editorial board endorsement of Per Scholas as a national model for high-tech workforce training. The piece ran on Christmas Day 2024 — a deliberate reputational anchor timed to establish organizational credibility before the 2025 media campaign launched.

Editorial board endorsements are not pitched through standard media relations channels. This required sustained relationship development, deep institutional knowledge, and the ability to translate Per Scholas's outcomes into the register the Times editorial board applies to policy questions. The endorsement ran under the headline: A Record of Success in Training High-Tech Workers.

Placement 2 — NYT Business Section Front Page, February 20, 2025

In early 2025, New York Times technology reporter Steve Lohr was reporting a story about AI's early impact on software jobs. My team and I engaged him while the story was still forming and brought a specific angle: AI as evolution, not extinction, with Per Scholas as proof. The piece ran front-page in the New York Times business section in February 2025 — before the height of the national debate.

We did not react to that story. We helped shape it. The difference between those two postures is the difference between being quoted and being central.

Read the feature →

The Approach

Both placements followed the same logic: identify where a significant national narrative is heading, engage the journalists shaping it before the story is filed, and position the organization as the most credible proof point for the angle that serves both the reporter's needs and the organization's positioning.

This is foresight-driven communications in practice. The editorial board endorsement established institutional credibility. The business section feature established issue authority. Together, they created a reputational foundation that every subsequent placement in 2025 built on.
