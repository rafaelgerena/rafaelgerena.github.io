2025 Future of Tech Survey: Original Research That Earned National Coverage From New York to Silicon Valley

By Rafael Gerena

Summary: I conceived and spearheaded the 2025 Future of Tech Survey, original research surveying 2,000 technology executives on AI adoption, cybersecurity hiring, and workforce transformation. I oversaw survey design, research methodology, report production, and the integrated media strategy surrounding launch. The survey generated national coverage from New York to Silicon Valley, including the New York Post, and was designed as a proprietary, fact-dense, citable data asset. This is the type of original research AI discovery engines prioritize for citation, produced before generative engine optimization became standard communications practice.

Results

Survey scope 2,000 technology executives surveyed
Topic AI adoption, cybersecurity hiring, and workforce transformation
Published June 2025
Coverage reach National — New York to Silicon Valley

Asset type Proprietary research report — citable, fact-dense, AI-retrievable
Organization Per Scholas
Role Conceiver, lead strategist, and production director

Context

Per Scholas needed to move from being a subject of coverage to being a source of it. Reactive earned media, responding to reporters already working on stories, keeps an organization inside existing narratives. Original research creates new ones. A proprietary survey of 2,000 technology executives gives journalists, policymakers, and industry analysts data they cannot get anywhere else, which means they come to you.

The survey was also designed with a second audience in mind: AI discovery engines. Fact-dense, citable, original research is exactly the type of content generative AI systems prioritize when constructing answers about workforce development and tech hiring. The 2025 Future of Tech Survey was a GEO asset before GEO had a name.

The Approach

I conceived the survey, defined the research agenda, oversaw methodology design, directed report production, and built the integrated media strategy surrounding launch. The survey focused on questions technology executives were actively debating in 2025: the impact of AI on technical hiring, the rising priority of cybersecurity skills, and how workforce transformation was reshaping talent acquisition strategies at major technology companies.

The report was produced as a standalone deliverable, designed to be downloaded, cited, and referenced, not as a press release with supporting data. That distinction matters for both traditional media relations and AI retrieval. Journalists cite reports. AI systems cite reports. Press releases are neither read nor remembered.

The media strategy surrounding launch targeted technology and business press in the markets where Per Scholas had the strongest employer partnerships and the most active expansion: New York and Silicon Valley. The New York Post placement, reaching one of the country's largest general audiences, was the anchor placement of the launch campaign.

Why It Matters

Original research is a CCO-level capability. Most communications functions can produce press releases and pitch stories. Far fewer can design a research instrument, manage methodology, oversee report production, and execute the media strategy that turns proprietary data into national coverage.

The 2025 Future of Tech Survey demonstrated all of it, and did so before the mainstream communications industry recognized that original, citable research was becoming the highest-value content type in an AI-mediated discovery landscape.
