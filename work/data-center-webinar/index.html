Data Centers in Demand: Convening Microsoft and TEKsystems Before the Narrative Peaked

By Rafael Gerena

Summary: I conceived, designed, and produced a panel discussion with Microsoft and TEKsystems leaders on building future-ready talent pipelines for the data center industry. I identified the AI data center workforce shortage as an emerging story before it broke into mainstream business coverage and built an event around it while the window was still open. The webinar preceded the national narrative on 340,000+ unfilled data center roles projected by end of 2026 and $680 billion in committed infrastructure spend by major tech companies in 2026 alone. A Forbes placement featuring the TEKsystems partnership followed in August 2025.

Context

By mid-2025, the AI infrastructure build-out had created a workforce crisis hiding in plain sight. Data centers require technicians, not just engineers, and the pipeline for that talent was drastically underprepared for the scale of investment underway. Projections now show 340,000 unfilled data center and related roles by end of 2026, while major tech companies including Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, and Microsoft have committed a record $680 billion to AI and data center infrastructure spending in 2026 alone.

Most communications teams were not covering this story yet. The mainstream business press had not caught up to the scale of the shortage. That gap was the opportunity.

The Approach

I identified the data center workforce shortage as an emerging narrative and designed a convening around it before the story broke into mainstream coverage. The webinar brought together Microsoft and TEKsystems leaders in a structured panel on building future-ready talent pipelines, with Per Scholas positioned as the solutions provider at the center of the conversation.

The panelist selection was deliberate. The Microsoft data center technician manager was a former police officer and Per Scholas graduate. His presence on the panel was not incidental. It was the proof point. A Microsoft regional program manager and two TEKsystems executives provided the enterprise credibility. The Per Scholas graduate provided the human evidence that the pipeline worked.

The event translated complex industry dynamics into accessible, outcome-driven storytelling for decision-makers across sectors. The goal was not a webinar with good attendance. It was a reusable narrative asset that enterprise partners, journalists, and policymakers could reference long after the event ended.

Why It Matters

The Forbes placement in August 2025, featuring the TEKsystems partnership as a model for skills-based hiring, was a direct downstream result of the relationships and narrative infrastructure built around this webinar. Enterprise convenings that are designed strategically do not end when the recording stops. They generate relationships, content, and coverage that compound over time.

This is foresight-driven communications in practice: identifying where a significant industry narrative is heading, building presence inside it before the moment becomes obvious, and creating an asset that earns returns long after the event date. The organizations that shape narratives are not the ones that respond fastest. They are the ones that showed up before the story had a name.
