CCO Elevation Is Accelerating: What New Research on Salary and Scope Reveals About Communications Authority

Originally published on LinkedIn, June 15, 2026.

The Wall Street Journal just ran a piece called "The Revenge of the Publicists." The headline is accurate, and the data behind it is sharper than the framing suggests.

Nearly half of Chief Communications Officers (CCOs) now report directly to their CEOs, 47%, up from 37% in 2015, per Korn Ferry. Median base salaries at Fortune 500 companies have risen 12 to 14 percent in two years, reaching $400,000 to $450,000, and about one in seven now earns $2 million or more in total compensation. Uber in May elevated its communications and policy head, Jill Hazelbaker, to a newly created president-level role overseeing corporate affairs across the company. Large companies including Gap, Peloton, and Reddit have recently hired their first CCOs.

None of this is accidental. It tracks a decade of pressure on the CEO's single most exposed variable: the speed at which reputational damage compounds. Anheuser-Busch InBev created its first CCO role in November 2023, after the Bud Light boycott. Goodyear's CCO joined from Philip Morris. The pattern is consistent: companies are not promoting PR into strategy. They are recognizing that narrative coherence at the speed of social media requires someone with institutional authority, not just institutional access.

The part of the piece that should interest every communications leader is what Korn Ferry and Zeno are reporting about scope. Roughly half of CCOs are now leading or partnering on AI change management, per Zeno Group's Clarity 2030 research. And comms chiefs are being explicitly tasked with managing how their organizations appear in ChatGPT, Claude, and other large language models. That is a generative engine optimization (GEO) responsibility landing in the CCO portfolio whether or not anyone has formally named it.

The salary data suggests the market has already priced in the expanded mandate. The resourcing has not: only 34% of CCOs say they are satisfied with their workload, per the Conference Board's CMO+CCO Meter, down 20 points since June 2025.

CCOs are being asked to hold the narrative across every channel that matters. The question is whether the infrastructure around them is being built at the same pace.
