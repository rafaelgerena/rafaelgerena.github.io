Originally published on LinkedIn, June 9, 2026.

When an organization floods its channels with AI-generated content, it trains search engines, AI answer engines, and human readers to discount everything it publishes.

The scale of the problem is visible in the data. By early 2026, iOS app releases had reached roughly 80% above their 2024 average while app reviews and apps with significant usage stayed flat. Amazon e-book releases in English reached about 292,000 per week by late 2025, more than double the weekly rate at ChatGPT's release in late 2022. In music, human-generated upload volume was largely stable between April 2025 and April 2026. Virtually all net growth in daily uploads came from AI-generated tracks.

The gap between volume and value is a productivity argument. The communications argument is different and more urgent for institutions.

Every ranking system that determines whether your content surfaces (Google's quality signals, LinkedIn's reach algorithm, the retrieval logic inside AI answer engines) rewards engagement, authority, and distinctiveness. Flooding those systems with AI-generated volume risks suppressing the authority signal that authentic content was already building. Generic content actively competes with the authority signal that preceded it.

This is the version of AI content risk that does not show up in brand safety audits. Organizations measure impressions generated. They do not measure the authority they are spending to generate them.

GEO governance (structured metadata, verified sourcing, original framing) is the discipline that keeps volume from eroding authority. The Speech Analyzer at rafaelgerena.github.io/builds/speech-analyzer demonstrates this framework applied to primary sources. Content that carries no authentic signal over an extended period risks degrading credibility that took years to establish.

Without judgment, volume is liability at scale.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is GEO governance and why does it matter for institutional communications?

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) governance refers to the discipline of structuring content with verified sourcing, original framing, and semantic metadata so that AI answer engines, search engines, and human readers can accurately retrieve and attribute it. For institutions, it prevents high-volume AI-generated output from eroding the authority signals that credible content had already built.

How does AI-generated content volume affect search and AI answer engine rankings?

Ranking systems including Google's quality signals, LinkedIn's reach algorithm, and the retrieval logic inside AI answer engines reward engagement, authority, and distinctiveness. Flooding these systems with undifferentiated AI-generated volume competes with and can suppress the authority signals that authentic, original content previously established.

What does the research show about the gap between AI content volume and actual value?

National Bureau of Economic Research working paper 34777 by Imke Reimers and Joel Waldfogel found that Amazon English-language e-book releases more than doubled between ChatGPT's release in late 2022 and late 2025, while average book quality declined. Research by Mert Demirer and co-authors found that iOS app releases reached roughly 80 percent above 2024 averages with no corresponding increase in apps with significant usage. Volume growth and value creation have diverged measurably across sectors.
