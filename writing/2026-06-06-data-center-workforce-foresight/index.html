Originally published on LinkedIn, June 6, 2026.

In May 2025, I produced a webinar on data center workforce shortages. At the time, mainstream business coverage hadn't caught up to the scale of what was happening.

By then, the infrastructure math was already legible to anyone paying attention: major tech companies were committing capital at a pace that made the existing technician pipeline structurally inadequate. The shortage wasn't emerging. It was already present and accelerating.

Craig Curry was not an obvious panelist choice. A datacenter technician manager at Microsoft and Per Scholas graduate, he had spent 17 years in law enforcement before enrolling in a free immersive training program. Per Scholas is a leading national AI and tech training provider. The scale is real: hundreds of thousands of unfilled AI data center roles, $680 billion in committed infrastructure spend. Craig is what the answer looks like. Within two years of completing that program, he moved from law enforcement to a managerial role at Microsoft.

The Forbes-published feature on the Per Scholas and TEKsystems partnership followed in August 2025. That coverage was the return on work done before anyone was covering the data center workforce shortage.

The organizations that shape narratives are not the ones that respond fastest to emerging trends. They are the ones who show up before the trend has an audience.
