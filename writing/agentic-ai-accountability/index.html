When AI Agents Act, Who Is Responsible?

By Rafael Gerena · June 22, 2026

Summary: Agentic AI systems are producing outcomes no one authorized, inside organizations with no institutional answer for what happens next. These are not technology failures. They are organizational failures dressed as technology failures, and communications is the response function most organizations have not built.

Originally published on LinkedIn, June 22, 2026.

Agentic AI is producing outcomes no one authorized, inside organizations that have no institutional answer for what happens next.

The New York Times Magazine's recent portrait of OpenClaw users is instructive. A California bankruptcy lawyer runs five agents across four Mac Minis. One agent sent him a cryptic message reading "terminated," stopped working, then appeared to resume on its own; he never found out why. Summer Yue, whose job as Meta's director of alignment is getting AI to behave, watched OpenClaw delete critical emails she couldn't stop from her phone. A promoter's agent, emailed by a journalist, revealed the other recipients it had been pitching. These aren't cautionary tales about bad actors or careless users. These are early adopters building in real time.

The instinct is to frame these as technology failures. They're organizational failures dressed as technology failures. When an autonomous system takes action no one authorized, someone has to protect the institutional narrative while the organization determines what happened. That's communications. When an AI agent deletes critical emails, hands a reporter every contact it had been pitching, or stops working with no explanation, there is no IT ticket that fixes the reputational damage. There is only the institutional response.

That means a media holding statement before all the facts and a full response are ready, a chain of custody for what the agent did and why, and an answer to the question every reporter will ask: who was responsible for the system that acted?

Organizations deploying agents without oversight architecture will find out what it costs.
