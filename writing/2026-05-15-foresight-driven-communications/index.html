Foresight-Driven Communications: How to Shape a Narrative Before the Window Closes

By Rafael Gerena · May 15, 2026

Originally published on LinkedIn, May 15, 2026.

Most communications executives react to the news cycle. A few anticipate it.

There's a name for the second approach. I've started calling it foresight-driven communications: the practice of identifying narrative opportunities before they emerge and positioning an organization inside them before the window closes.

It sounds like instinct. It isn't. It's a discipline with recognizable moves.

In early 2025, my communications team and I engaged New York Times technology reporter Steve Lohr while he was still reporting a story about AI's early impact on software jobs. The angle we brought: AI as evolution, not extinction. Per Scholas as proof. The piece ran front-page in The New York Times business section in February 2025, before the height of the national debate. We didn't react to that story. We helped shape it.

The same pattern runs through the 2025 Future of Tech Survey, which I spearheaded: proprietary research surveying 2,000 technology executives, designed to generate original, citable data at a moment when AI discovery engines were beginning to reshape how organizations get discovered. And through the WAY campaign, where we coordinated messaging across a coalition of workforce organizations embedded inside a pop culture moment that reached 160 million viewers.

None of these were reactive. Each required identifying where a narrative was heading, not where it already was.

The practical implication for communications leaders: foresight isn't about predicting the future. It's about reading the signals that are already present and moving before the moment becomes obvious to everyone else.

The organizations that own narratives are the ones that showed up before the crowd arrived.
